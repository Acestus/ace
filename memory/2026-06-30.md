### Flight Change Guidance (Specific Case)

If you're changing the flight time to a different day but staying on the same route, follow these steps:

1. **Contact the airline's customer service**: Reach out directly to explain your situation and request assistance. Be prepared to provide your booking confirmation and any relevant details about your travel plans.

2. **Request a time change**: Clearly state that you'd like to change the flight time to a different day while keeping the same route. Ask about availability and any potential fees or restrictions.

3. **Explore alternative options**: If the airline cannot accommodate your request, consider rebooking with a different airline or using a flight change service. These services often have more flexibility and can help find suitable alternatives.

4. **Check refund policies**: If the change is not possible, review the airline's refund policies. You may be eligible for a refund or compensation for the inconvenience caused.

5. **Stay persistent and communicate**: Keep communication open with the airline to increase your chances of finding a suitable solution. Be patient and persistent, as airlines may have limited availability for certain dates and times.

By following these steps, you can increase your chances of successfully changing your flight time while staying on the same route.

### Flight Change Guidance (Non-Transferable Flight)

If you're dealing with a non-transferable flight, the airline might still be able to assist you, especially if you speak with the right person or department. Here's what you can do:

1. **Contact the airline's customer service**: Reach out directly to explain your situation and request assistance. Be prepared to provide your booking confirmation and any relevant details about your travel plans.

2. **Speak with a supervisor or manager**: If the standard customer service is unable to assist, ask to speak with a supervisor or manager. They may have more authority to make changes or find alternative solutions.

3. **Request a manual change**: Clearly state that you'd like to change your flight, even though it's non-transferable. Ask about any potential fees or restrictions and inquire about availability for the new date and time.

4. **Explore alternative options**: If the airline cannot accommodate your request, consider rebooking with a different airline or using a flight change service. These services often have more flexibility and can help find suitable alternatives.

5. **Check refund policies**: If the change is not possible, review the airline's refund policies. You may be eligible for a refund or compensation for the inconvenience caused.

6. **Stay persistent and communicate**: Keep communication open with the airline to increase your chances of finding a suitable solution. Be patient and persistent, as airlines may have limited availability for certain dates and times.

By following these steps, you can increase your chances of successfully changing your flight, even if it's non-transferable.
