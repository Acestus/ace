## Notes

### Lede

Build is June 2–3. The narrative is locked: cloud architect on the platform layer, pulling AI agents into everything. The philosophy anchor is OpenClaw (Peter Steinberger, now at OpenAI) — "AI that actually does things." The concrete image: doctor doing rounds, agents supporting, optimizing time. Not selling at the floor — making connections. The $50 Fabric CI/CD review comes up only if it fits naturally.

### Headline

> "Cloud architect focused on Azure and Fabric — I spend most of my time on the platform layer, and I've been pulling AI agents into everything."

### When they ask "what kind of AI work?"

> "I'm a big fan of Peter Steinberger and what he built with OpenClaw — the idea that AI should actually *do* things, not just chat. I implement that philosophy both personally and professionally. I have a workflow toolkit where I put myself in the doctor role doing rounds — the agents are supporting me, and I'm optimizing the time."

### When they ask "what do you build professionally?"

> "Platform engineering on Azure — IaC, Fabric data pipelines, identity, observability. The kind of work that makes everything else deployable and repeatable."

### If the offer comes up naturally

> "I'm doing a small Fabric CI/CD readiness review — fixed scope, $50. For teams who want Git-backed deployments without breaking schedules or environment settings."
