## Notes

### Lede

Microsoft Build is Tuesday. The goal isn't to sell anything or land a specific outcome — it's to spend 10 minutes at every booth, say hi to as many people as possible, and start building a reputation as a known entity in the Microsoft/Azure/Fabric community. This is a long-game play: marathon, not a sprint. Success means leaving with a list of people met and a system to follow up with all of them.

### Deliverables (before Tuesday)

1. **Pitch line** — one crisp sentence, memorable, conversation-opening
2. **Mugshot card** — shareable image: photo + name + email + one-liner. People snap a photo, done. No NFC trust required.
3. **Voice capture workflow** — "Hey Siri, make a note: met [name], [context]" → feeds into Notion CRM (ACE-6)
4. **Notion CRM ready** (ACE-6) — the follow-up backend; contacts land here after the event

### Pitch line

> "Cloud architect focused on Azure and Fabric. I spend most of my time on the platform layer — and I've been pulling AI agents into everything."

- **Contact list** — name, company/role, what you talked about, LinkedIn URL
- **Re-touch message** — warm "hey, remember me from Build" note, small talk anchor, no hard sell
- **Ongoing cadence** — periodic check-ins to stay on their radar over time
- **Backend** — ACE-6 Notion CRM holds all of this; one row per person met

### Success criteria

Leave the floor with a list of people met and a working system to follow up with all of them within 48h.

### Strategy

No specific booth targets — just rounds. Spend ~10 min per stop, introduce, collect, move. Marathon play: the goal is to become a known face in the community over time, not to close anything Tuesday.
