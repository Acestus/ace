## Description

Scope the Make $50 project, identify the smallest viable path, and capture assumptions, dependencies, and next steps.

## Notes

### Lede

The goal is not to build a business plan; it is to get to the first $50 as fast as possible using a realistic online offer. The operator has time and budget to invest, but wants a simple path that turns existing cloud-architect skills into a first sale.

### Status

Current scope points to a service-first approach: sell a small, concrete remote offer online instead of trying to launch a full product.

### Next

Define the smallest paid offer, identify the first buyer channel, and write down the first action that can produce a sale.

### Offer focus

Fabric CI/CD for cloud-native deployment is the first offer. The service should help a customer:

- get Fabric artifacts into Git,
- wire CI/CD so changes deploy repeatably,
- keep schedules and environment settings intact,
- and reduce manual drift during releases.

### Why this angle

It aligns with your portfolio, is specific enough to sell, and is concrete enough to scope into a fixed deliverable.

### Offer package

Working name: **Fabric CI/CD readiness review**

Price target: **$50**

Deliverable:

- a short intake call,
- a review of the Fabric workspace and repo flow,
- a checklist of the biggest CI/CD gaps,
- and the top 3 fixes to get to repeatable deployment.

### Draft outreach

Hey — I saw you’re working in Fabric. I’m doing a small Fabric CI/CD readiness review for teams that want Git-backed deployments without breaking schedules or environment settings. If you want, I can send a quick checklist and call out the top 3 gaps I’d look for.

### Buyer channel

The first buyers are **Microsoft Build attendees** — people already at the event who are likely to care about Fabric and cloud-native CI/CD.

### Immediate tactic

Use the event to find people who are actively working in Fabric and offer a small, fixed-scope CI/CD review or deployment setup.

### Success criteria

A successful first review is a successful sale.

### Blocker / risk

The biggest risk is that Build attendees may not show interest in a paid readiness review.

### First outreach action

Reach out on LinkedIn to Build attendees who are working in Fabric and ask if they want a short CI/CD readiness review.

### Portfolio signal

Your portfolio points strongly at Azure, Microsoft Fabric, cloud platform engineering, FinOps, and agentic workflow tooling. The clearest money-making path from that expertise is a small productized service, not a general-purpose consumer product.

### Recommendation

Start with a narrow offer such as a cloud architecture / cost / capacity review, then package it as a fixed-scope deliverable you can sell online.
